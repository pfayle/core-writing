Action

Your choices are limited. You either participate or you don't, and you either become informed or you don't:

  1. Continue to participate in normal life without understanding, aware you are seen as the primary obstacle, trying to keep your head down or fighting blindly.
  2. Play a part in experiments that can initiate change, through conversation, labour or finance, but without understanding them or endorsing any associated visions.
  3. Actively endorse the status quo, defending why you do not want substantial change.
  4. Position yourself to take an informed part in experiments, taking these ideas, adapting them, adding your own, and taking the lead.

Today's world is not a satisfactory place, and there are many things to be done in many places before it can be. In this context, I find it possible to get asymptotically close to personal satisfaction by walking the line of how much I can do and cope with effectively while leaving enough room for others to work towards the same goals.

Consider how you're different to me and others.

If you're fundamentally different in not feeling empathy, I would recommend you choose to adopt it as a foundational value anyway and accept the need to learn about what others understand naturally. You will often end up understanding it better – we would not be in this position if people understood their own empathy well.

If you're different and don't know why, I would recommend being cautious, so that you can explore until you can understand yourself, and how you would like to be accommodated, better.

To be understood is not to be powerless. Not to understand, is.

If you're not different, I recommend looking for subtler and more fulfilling ways to express your individuality than exaggerating chimerical differences. Full openness is most often impractical, but in the long run a relatable, fallible but dauntless example that people can follow is of more use than an inimitable hero.

Start by revisiting what you need to know, or taking another look at how you can participate in experiments.
